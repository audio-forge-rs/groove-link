# Texas Women

*Texas cities and towns that share a name with a woman. Seven lines each — could be about the town, could be about the girl. You decide.*

---

### Victoria — pop. ~66,000

1. She was regal before anyone else showed up, founded on Spanish land and Catholic stone
2. I occupied her for a season but she was never really mine
3. Thirty miles from the Gulf and I could taste the salt on everything she said
4. Old-world name, old-world silence — she carries history in her bones
5. I drove through on a Sunday and the church bells told me I was late
6. The river runs right through her and she never once tried to stop it
7. Victoria doesn't wait — she was already somebody before I learned her name

### Katy — pop. ~30,000

1. Named for a railroad that dropped half its letters and kept moving west
2. The hurricane swept her clean in 1900 and she just built it back in concrete
3. She's resilient and productive and I was neither of those things
4. Friday night lights and rice fields — Katy knows what matters and it ain't you
5. I missed my stop and she was already three counties behind me
6. Everybody out past Houston ends up at Katy whether they meant to or not
7. She bounced back from every storm and I'm still standing in the last one

### Melissa — pop. ~24,000

1. Even her father's identity is disputed — she never had solid ground
2. They found bones beneath her dated nine thousand years before Christ
3. A tornado took her in '21, a fire took her in '29, and she's richer now than all of us
4. Population dropped to 285 and nobody said her name for fifty years
5. I knew a Melissa once who was nearly destroyed twice and came back both times sharper
6. She went from nothing to a hundred thirty-seven thousand a year and I went the other direction
7. Some women get leveled and rebuild in stone — I just got leveled

### Addison — pop. ~17,000

1. More restaurants per capita than anywhere in America and I still ate alone
2. Daytime population swells to a hundred twenty thousand — at night she empties out
3. She voted to legalize everything just to keep people coming back
4. An airport covers an eighth of her body and she doesn't mind the noise
5. Addison is always hosting, always more than she appears, always someone else by morning
6. I mistook her warmth for something personal — turned out she's like that with everyone
7. Surrounded on all sides by something bigger and she still kept her own name

### Alice — pop. ~17,500

1. Hub City — equidistant from everywhere, belonging to no one
2. Birthplace of Tejano music and two Nobel Prize winners, which is more than I ever gave her
3. Named for a cattle king's daughter who never had to ask for anything
4. A hundred oil companies and I couldn't find a single reason to stay
5. Alice sits right in the center and lets the rest of Texas come to her
6. I drove through her on the way to somewhere else and that's the whole story
7. She's centrally located and surprisingly brilliant and I was passing through

### Donna — pop. ~17,000

1. Named for a twenty-one-year-old divorcée whose daddy bought her twenty-three thousand acres
2. City with a heart in the heart of the valley — she'll tell you that herself
3. The first settlers came to escape persecution for loving who they loved
4. The Texas Rangers showed up because the politics got physical
5. Donna doesn't back down and I never learned not to push
6. She was given everything and still earned every bit of respect she got
7. I had heart but not the acreage — Donna needed both

### Mercedes — pop. ~16,000

1. Queen City of the Valley — she wore the title like it was owed to her
2. Founded on a Spanish land grant from 1790 and she still holds the deed
3. She made beautiful things by hand — boots that cost more than my truck
4. Ranchers settled her in the 1770s and she hasn't changed her mind since
5. Mercedes has deep roots and I have shallow pockets
6. I couldn't afford her and she never pretended I could
7. Some women are born on granted land — the rest of us just lease

### Josephine — pop. ~12,000

1. She arrived quietly and suddenly everyone wants to be near her
2. Four hundred thirty-four percent growth and I'm still shrinking
3. She used to be nothing east of Dallas and now she's the fastest thing in Collin County
4. I watched Josephine explode from a distance like a man watching a house he sold triple in value
5. Quiet name, loud trajectory — she outgrew me before I noticed
6. Population surging fifteen percent a year and I can't gain a single pound of purpose
7. Josephine doesn't need you to discover her — she was already on her way

### Selma — pop. ~12,000

1. She'll slow you down and take your money — Steve Earle wrote a song about it
2. German pioneers founded her in 1847 and she still has that stubborn streak
3. Population dropped to a hundred and she sat there for decades waiting for the interstate
4. Texas Monthly called her the perfect speed trap and she took it as a compliment
5. I got pulled over in Selma doing seventy-three in a sixty and she didn't blink
6. Horse racing and highway robbery — Selma's got a wild side under all that jurisdiction
7. She grew six hundred percent once people had no choice but to pass through her

### Venus — pop. ~10,000

1. Originally named Gossip until they gave her something grander in the 1880s
2. She was one of the most prosperous towns in the county and then the Depression left her hollow
3. One drugstore and empty buildings — Venus after the money left
4. Population tripled in ten years and everybody acts like they knew her all along
5. I loved a Venus once who used to go by a different name and I preferred the first one
6. She was called Gossip, she was prosperous, she was abandoned, and now she's popular again
7. Some women get renamed when they become beautiful — the ugly name was more honest

### Electra — pop. ~2,200

1. Named for a spoiled daughter who spent a million dollars in a single day at Neiman Marcus
2. The oil gusher was an April Fool's joke that turned out to be true
3. She made people rich and nobody believed the news about her
4. Pump Jack Capital of Texas — she'll drain you dry and call it industry
5. Electra is extravagant and I am the opposite of extravagant
6. Ten million barrels between 1911 and the twenties and then the wells went quiet
7. I was the joke that turned out to be serious — she was the fortune I walked past

### Edna — pop. ~6,000

1. Named for the railroad man's daughter — always someone's afterthought until she became the county seat
2. She replaced someone older and took the seat of power without apology
3. Hometown of Stone Cold Steve Austin, which tells you everything about her temperament
4. Gateway to Lake Texana, which covers the drowned town she replaced
5. The Flag City of Texas — Edna loves a symbol more than the thing itself
6. I lost my seat to an Edna once and she didn't even know my name
7. Eleven thousand acres of water over the bones of what came before her

### Crystal — pop. ~6,000

1. Named for the water — clear artesian wells that come up clean through fractured rock
2. Spinach Capital of the World with a statue of Popeye across from city hall
3. She held an internment camp during the war and nobody talks about it at dinner
4. Birthplace of a revolution she didn't ask to mother
5. Crystal is clear and strong and complicated and she's been through things you can't see
6. I thought I could see right through her — turns out the clarity was one-directional
7. Some women are named for what's underneath them and it's never what you expect

### Elsa — pop. ~6,700

1. Named for a Scotsman's wife who probably never set foot in the Rio Grande Valley
2. Birthplace of Ro-Tel canned tomatoes, 1943 — she started something everyone knows
3. Tomato festival on Cinco de Mayo — she celebrates in her own time zone
4. She's warm, she's in the citrus belt, and she made me cry into my chili
5. Heart of the vegetable country and I am mostly processed food
6. A Scottish name in a Mexican valley — Elsa never fit and never tried to
7. She canned what was fresh and shipped it everywhere and I'm still spoiling on the vine

### Lorena — pop. ~1,800

1. Population was two hundred forty-two in the fifties and she just waited
2. Named for a prominent man's daughter who probably never drove the thirteen miles from Waco
3. Interstate 35 revived her and I don't have a highway coming
4. She's patient, she waited decades, and the road eventually came to her
5. I was the two-lane blacktop — Lorena needed the interstate
6. She grew slowly and steadily while I burned fast and went nowhere
7. Some women just wait for the right highway and it always comes — I'm still on the shoulder

### Aurora — pop. ~1,400

1. Famous for a UFO that hit Judge Proctor's windmill in 1897
2. The pilot was not of this world and they buried him in the local cemetery
3. She's mysterious, she harbors secrets, and she'll never tell
4. The cemetery association refused all requests to exhume — Aurora keeps what's hers
5. I crashed into her like something from another world and she just marked the grave
6. Wise County, northwest corner, and nobody goes there unless they're looking for something gone
7. Aurora means dawn but the only famous thing about her happened at impact

### Natalia — pop. ~1,300

1. Her name was misspelled on the post office records and never corrected
2. Her father built the biggest dam west of the Mississippi and drowned on the Lusitania
3. She was named wrong, her father was lost at sea, and she fed hundreds anyway
4. The canning plant employed five hundred people in peak season and she didn't get a dime
5. Natalia is someone's daughter, misspelled and misfiled, doing the work regardless
6. I loved a Natalia once whose name I kept getting wrong and she stopped correcting me
7. Some women carry a wrong name their whole life and make it sound right

### Blossom — pop. ~1,500

1. A hundred mineral wells advertised as cure-alls — she promised healing she couldn't deliver
2. She educated a future vice president and nobody remembers
3. Three banks in a town of fifteen hundred — Blossom believed in her own potential
4. Originally Blossom Prairie, which is prettier, but nobody uses the whole name anymore
5. The healing waters dried up and the banks closed and the prairie is just a word now
6. I went to Blossom looking for a cure and found a cemetery and a post office
7. Some women bloom on a prairie and some women are the prairie after the bloom

### Lolita — pop. ~520

1. Named for a Texas Revolution veteran's granddaughter — the name carries baggage she didn't ask for
2. She peaked at four hundred sixty-two in 1969 and has been losing people since
3. First settled in the 1840s by a man who ran a plantation — her foundations are complicated
4. Revolutionary blood in a coastal bend town that nobody visits on purpose
5. I knew a Lolita once and the name preceded her into every room like a warning
6. She peaked young and I arrived late and the timing was the whole problem
7. Some names ruin a town and some towns ruin a name — Lolita got both

### Irene — pop. ~85

1. The name means peace in Greek and the population means peace in practice
2. Twelve miles southeast of Hillsboro — an unincorporated quiet I couldn't afford
3. She drifted from a hundred seventy to eighty-six and never raised her voice about it
4. Irene has her own zip code and that's more than I have
5. She's barely there but she's still on the map, which is more than I can say for us
6. I went looking for peace and found an Irene with a post office and nothing else
7. Population eighty-five and falling — Irene is the sound of Texas forgetting
